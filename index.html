01 — History

About GT40
‍

Born from Henry Ford's obsession to defeat Ferrari at Le Mans, the GT40 was engineered with one goal: win at all costs. Development began in 1963, blending American muscle with European racing know-how. Powered by a massive 4.7-liter V8 mid-mounted behind the cockpit, it rewrote the rulebook on endurance racing.

After two years of setbacks, the GT40 delivered in 1966 — dominating Le Mans with a historic 1-2-3 finish, ending Ferrari's six-year winning streak. It remains one of the greatest upsets in motorsport history.

02 — Design

A Feast
For The Eyes

Designed by Eric Broadley and refined by Ford Advanced Vehicles, the GT40's low-slung body sits just 40 inches tall — giving it its name. Every surface was shaped by aerodynamic necessity: the long nose, the sharp tail, the iconic louvered hood vents all exist to keep the car planted at 200+ mph.
